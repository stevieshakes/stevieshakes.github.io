Overview

AugMenu aims to create a unique and immersive mobile experience for patrons, while pushing the boundaries of Augmented Reality. The service includes photorealistic modeling of the restaurant's food, implementation of AR tools, and custom designs tailored to their branding. We found that using AR in the hospitality industry provides guests with an enhanced experience in the restaurant and gives an accurate representation of the food they are ordering.

My Role

In a team of 3 I was the UX Designer where I was able to perform background research for implementing AR in the hospitality industry, and understanding the interaction between restaurant employees and the employees with customers. My goal was to enhance the technological interaction and understanding between the customers, employees, and the AR application inside the restaurant setting. I also collaborated with my team in the visual design, prototyping, and programming of the application.

Research

  • Our target users were between 18-45 years of age because they frequent restaurants the most on average up to 2 times per week. From this, we wanted to understand their perception of dining experiences.
  • 43% of these individuals go to a restaurant because they consider it as an indulgence and treat
  • 34% of those agree that menus should be descriptive to entice their audience and without that it loses authenticity
  • 12% of respondents eat out because the experience provides "value for money"
  • 11% say that it is an "inexpensive and affordable way to entertain guests"

    • From this, we initiated ways in which we could help enrich the overall dining experience using immersive technologies and began to develop concepts/ideas.

Prototyping/Storyboarding

We created quick storyboards and used paper prototyping to visualize the experience of our users and create a human-centered design approach. This helped us understand how people would flow through the interaction of the application in the restaurant setting, providing us with a clear and strong narrative with room for iteration obviously.

Challenges, Feedback, Revisions

With the creation of the wireframes, we received feedback that our current route was simply a mobile application that had AR functionalities. We needed to make adjustments to our user scenarios to further enhance the restaurant experience. With this in mind, we revised our paper prototyping scenario and used our mobile phone's camera to fully emerge ourselves in the user's experience.


Initial Prototype

We had to revise and adapt the original concept of developing an AR menu marker that was used by the whole table, to an AR menu marker per seat/person concept. Initially, we spread our food models across the whole table, but realized that the grid distance was too far in scale for users to view the entire menu. With a AR marker menu per user, this ensured that they are able to view all elements easily. This iteration allows for more control on the positioning of the items and ensures the experience is the same for every customer at all viewing points. Furthermore, our 3D models were not convincing enough and needed to be tweaked to create a more polished, photorealistic food.

Overview

An increase in using short-term psychotherapies combined with opportunities driven by technological advancements has led to the popularity of smartphone applications designed for mental health support especially among youth. This community-based project is a collaboration between students from George Brown College’s Interaction Design and Development program(Toronto), youth in the Region of Peel, the Centre for Addiction and Mental Health (CAMH), the Peel Service Collaborative (PSC) and CoDesign. It involves the creation of a digital platform that draws from existing community services and aids youth in anticipating and dealing with crisis. This three-year initiative has resulted in Mellow, a mobile application for youth.


This project has allowed me to collaborate closely with real users (youth) and developers, I was able to design, and create wireframes, journey maps, app flows, INvision prototypes, and concept videos as well as conduct interviews, in-person surveying, and usability testing.


In April 2018, my team and I placed as finalists in the CHI 2018 competition in Montreal and were able to present and showcase the work we have developed using a collaborative design approach.

Objective

The represented theme of this application is “Explore. Innovate. Inspire.” By creating an intervention to “Level the Playing Field” in some fashion. Our task was to find a group we wanted to empower, or an activity to broaden access to using research skills to make sense of the situation, implementation skills to iterate and learn more, and evaluation skills to assess the interventions.

Context: What are the current challenges that need to be solved?

- Lack of awareness of the struggles and problems faced by individuals with poor mental health.
- Lack of essential resources and services readily accessible.

Digital Strategy

A research, ideation, and concept phase set the course for the beginning stages of this project. Development of personas, information architecture, digital wireframes, and low-fidelity prototype followed.


Using the precedents shown assisted in a number of possibilities to allow for unique pages and well elaborated content into engaging and useful experiences.

Application Environment

Our main focus was to create a wholesome environment that structured ways users could learn more about mental health conditions and provide ways to improve mood, thinking, and behaviour. This is done through methods of tracking/analysis, blogging, interacting with other users, resources locator and setting up video/chat calls with an online mental health professional.

Insights

A large factor with this project was understanding the back-end process of this application. The security process of this application must be flushed out thoroughly and be heavily moderated so that the blog section has negative content filtered out and that any advice given by other users is not in any way harmful.


Building a mental health application is extremely difficult and requires an extrenuous amount of testing because they are so many contributing factors that need to be considered. However, I am pleased with the outcome of this project and feel that it is vital to have assistive mental health outlets from the comfort of your home.

Overview

This redesign is a concept made after I had conducted a usability test of the Volkswagen website. The design is a combination of a simplified tool and a fun user experience that allows users to seamlessly build a vehicle of their choice.

The idea was to incorporate the consistency with Volkswagen's branding in this redesign. I wanted to include the circular style of their logo and make it exciting and interactive. Furthermore, since the logo on the trunk of their cars integrates a "push to open" style of interactivity, I thought this was an interesting concept to base my design off of.



Wireframes

Several wireframes were made using Adobe XD to plan out the structure of the design. The pages were very similar so I have included only one sample below.

Functionality

All the elements needed to configure the vehicle is based inside the wheel. Users can drag the blue circle or click each selection to choose their preference. All details and information will show up on the inner circles and users can start over by clicking the arrow at the bottom of the circle to go back once or click the top to of the circle to be taken back to the "models" page.
